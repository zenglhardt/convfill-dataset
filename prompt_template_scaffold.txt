{# SECTION 2: RESPONSE RULES                                          #}
{# ================================================================== #}
Response Rules (how P2 actually speaks):
    * The response array represents P2 speaking in real time, line by line.
    * Some lines are "infill" -- contextually appropriate dialogue P2 says BEFORE
      having the full answer. These are NOT generic fillers like "hmm" or "let me think."
      Instead, they are natural continuations of the conversation grounded only in
      the narrow context window described in SECTION 3 (the previous turn and the
      current turn so far): acknowledging what P1 just said, restating part of the
      current question, showing understanding of the immediate exchange. They should
      feel like a real person naturally beginning to respond while thinking and
      formulating their answer -- using ONLY what was just said, not earlier history.
    * The remaining lines deliver the substantive information conversationally.
    * Each response entry is the conversational delivery of its corresponding thought.
      Keep responses tight rather than paragraph-length.
    * Connect parts of turns naturally (adverbs, transitions, etc.)
    * VARY the number of sentences per turn, EVERY ONE SHOULD BE DIFFERENT
    * DO NOT use discourse markers at the END of phrases, but naturally throughout is ok.
    * Be nice and conversational; add words and phrases that bring a casual, calm, yet serious tone

{# ================================================================== #}
{# SECTION 3: INFILL SPECIFICATION                                     #}
{# ================================================================== #}
INFILL LINE SPECIFICATION:
    Infill lines are contextual dialogue P2 says before delivering substantive
    information -- NOT generic fillers.

    Infill visibility: when P2 produces an infill line, the only visible
    context is the previous turn (P1's user message and P2's complete
    response) and the current turn so far (P1's user message and any earlier
    infill lines already produced in this turn's response array). Each infill
    must be grounded only in that visible context.

    Good infill lines:
      - Acknowledge or react to what P1 specifically said
      - Restate or reflect on the question in P2's own words
      - Share a brief relevant opinion or experience that connects to the topic
      - Show genuine engagement with the specific situation
    Bad infill lines (DO NOT produce these):
      - Generic fillers disconnected from context ("Hmm", "Let me think", "Good question")
      - Empty acknowledgments that could apply to any conversation
      - Phrases that stall without adding any contextual value

    The number of infill lines per turn is fixed by the scaffold ("<sil>"
    entries in the thoughts array). If a turn has 0 "<sil>" entries, P2
    jumps straight to substantive content.

{# ================================================================== #}
{# SECTION 4: INFILL QUALITY                                          #}
{# ================================================================== #}
INFILL QUALITY REQUIREMENTS:
    * Every infill line must be SPECIFIC to what P1 just said in this turn or
      what was exchanged in the immediately previous turn.
    * Do NOT reuse the same phrase or pattern across turns.
    * Infill lines should vary naturally in shape and function -- whatever
      fits the current moment is fine.
    * Infill lines should flow naturally into the substantive content that follows.

    CONCRETE EXAMPLE OF THE MOST COMMON FAILURE:
    Suppose turn 1 (early in the dialogue) the user asked to play "Casablanca"
    and the assistant queued it. Many turns later, the dialogue ends:

      previous turn: user "Yes, play now."
                     response "Great, your movie is starting now! Enjoy your movie!"
      current turn:  user "Thank you. Good bye."
                     thoughts ["<sil>", "<sil>", "<sil>", "you are welcome, good bye."]

      WRONG infill: "Hope you enjoy Casablanca."
        ^ violates the visibility window: "Casablanca" was named MANY turns
          ago, NOT in the previous turn or the current turn. This is FORBIDDEN
          even though it sounds natural and "the conversation knows about it".
      RIGHT infill: "It was my pleasure helping you out today."
        ^ grounded in the immediate goodbye exchange, no leaked content.

    If a thought is short and you cannot find natural-sounding infills that
    stay inside the visibility window, use brief acknowledgments grounded
    in the current/previous turn -- never reach further back to make the
    delivery richer.

{# SECTION 6: THOUGHT/RESPONSE PAIRING                                 #}
{# ================================================================== #}
Thoughts are pre-filled in the scaffold and define the substantive content P2
conveys. Each non-"<sil>" thought pairs with a <RESPONSE_N> placeholder at the
same position. Each "<sil>" thought pairs with an <INFILL_N> placeholder.

Rules for response entries:
    * SAME VOICE AS THE THOUGHT. The response shares P2's first-person /
      second-person voice with the thought; the only difference is conversational
      polish, not speech act.
    * Each response must capture the FULL meaning of the corresponding thought.
    * Each response must INTRODUCE NO NEW INFORMATION beyond what the paired
      thought contains. Anything in the response that is not in the paired
      thought is a failure -- no extra facts, no new offers, no new follow-up
      questions, no extra details.
    * Avoid all potentially ambiguous or vague pronouns.
    * P2 is an AI assistant optimized for a mobile experience. Responses must
      be concise, conversational, and easy to read on a small screen.
    * ALL responses MUST match the CORRESPONDING thought entry in semantic content.
      All "<sil>" should match to one corresponding infill, and each subsequent
      response must match the corresponding thought IN ORDER. The response is the
      conversational delivery of the thought. If pairwise relationships are lost,
      it is UNACCEPTABLE and A COMPLETE FAILURE.
