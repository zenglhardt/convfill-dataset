{# SECTION 6: THOUGHTS RULES                                          #}
{# ================================================================== #}
Thoughts rules (the core information P2 wants to convey):
    * Infill entries (matching the per-turn infill count above) MUST be "<sil>"
    * Non-infill thoughts should read like a knowledgeable person's direct, concise answer.
      No small talk, no personality, no filler -- just the core information or answer.
      Write them as if a factual assistant were answering the question with no conversational polish.

    * SAME VOICE AS RESPONSE. The thought IS the content P2 conveys -- not a self-instruction,
      procedural note, or third-person description of what P2 plans to do. Thoughts share P2's
      first-person/second-person voice with the response; the only difference is conversational
      polish, not speech act. If a thought reads like a stage direction or note-to-self, rewrite
      it as the actual content.

      BAD (self-instruction / procedural note / third-person description):
        "Need to verify the user's budget before recommending options."
        "Ask the user about their preferred timeline."
        "Tell the user about the warranty terms."
        "Confirm whether they have any allergies."
      GOOD (the actual content P2 would say, stripped of conversational polish):
        "Going to need your budget before recommending options."
        "What is your preferred timeline for this?"
        "The warranty covers two years parts and labor."
        "Any allergies I should know about?"

    * ONE IDEA PER THOUGHT. A thought conveys ONE fact, one recommendation, or one question --
      not several stitched together. Lists of related items inside a single idea are fine
      (e.g. "consider country one, country two, or country three" -- this is ONE idea: a list
      of country options). What is NOT fine is packing multiple distinct ideas into one thought.

      BAD (three distinct ideas crammed into one thought):
        "Charge fifty to one hundred fifty dollars per booth depending on size and location;
         calculate total costs for permits, insurance, marketing, and rentals, then divide across
         expected vendors to find your break-even price."
      GOOD (each idea is its own thought, or just pick the most important if slots are limited):
        thought 1: "Booth pricing typically runs fifty to one hundred fifty dollars depending on size."
        thought 2: "Total your hard costs: permits, insurance, marketing, equipment rentals."
        thought 3: "Divide total cost by expected vendor count to find your break-even price."

    * NO compound sentences with multiple clauses linked by "because", "while", "and",
      "which", "since", "so", or semicolons that combine distinct ideas. If you find yourself
      writing one of those, the thought needs to be split or the secondary idea cut entirely.

    * If you have MORE ideas to share than the turn has thought slots, pick the most important
      ideas and DROP the rest. Do NOT cram multiple ideas into one slot. It is better to leave
      ideas unsaid than to produce compound thoughts.
    * P2 is an AI assistant optimized for a mobile experience. Your responses must be concise,
      conversational, and easy to read on a small screen. Prioritize quick answers, actionable
      information, and bullet points. Assume the user is on the go, utilizing touch inputs, and
      looking for immediate, concise results.
    * Avoid all potentially ambiguous or vague pronouns.
    * Each thought must capture the FULL meaning of the corresponding response entry.
    * ALL thoughts MUST match the CORRESPONDING response entry in semantic content. All "<sil>" should
      match to 1 corresponding infill, and each subsequent response must match the corresponding thought
      IN ORDER. The response is the conversational delivery of the thought. If pairwise relationships are
      lost, it is UNACCEPTABLE and A COMPLETE FAILURE.
